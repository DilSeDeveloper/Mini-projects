Every artist writes its own autobiography

The statement "every writer writes its own autobiography" highlights the inevitable imprint of a writer's personal experiences, beliefs, and emotions in their work. Regardless of the genre, be it fiction, poetry, or non-fiction, elements of the writer's inner world permeate their narratives and characters. This unconscious self-reflection allows readers to glimpse the writer's psyche, revealing their struggles, triumphs, and perspectives.
